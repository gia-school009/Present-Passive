The Present Passive Voice

Course: Grammar for High School Students

What is the Passive Voice?

In a normal (active) sentence, the subject performs the action.
Example: The boy kicks the ball.

In a passive sentence, the subject receives the action. The focus is on the object of the action.

Example: The ball is kicked by the boy.

Notice how the object of the active sentence ("the ball") becomes the subject of the passive sentence.

When Do We Use the Passive Voice?

  • When the person or thing doing the action (the agent) is unknown.

    My motorbike was stolen. (We don't know who stole it.)

  • When the agent is unimportant or obvious.

    The roads are being repaired. (It's obvious that workers are repairing them.)

  • To sound more formal or scientific.

    The water samples were analyzed in the lab.

  • When the action or result is more important than the agent.

    Angkor Wat was built in the 12th century.

Present Tense Passives

1. Present Simple Passive

Used for facts, habits, and regular occurrences.

Form: am / is / are + past participle

Fresh seafood is sold at Phsar Leu market every morning.

Many mangoes are grown in Kampong Speu province.

I am invited to the wedding ceremony.

2. Present Continuous Passive

Used for actions happening right now.

Form: am / is / are + being + past participle

A new condominium is being built in Phnom Penh.

The streets are being cleaned for the upcoming holiday.

3. Present Perfect Passive

Used for past actions with a result in the present, or for actions in an unfinished period of time.

Form: has / have + been + past participle

The Cambodia-China Friendship Bridge has been completed.

Many new restaurants have been opened in Siem Reap this year.

4. Present Perfect Continuous Passive

This tense is very rare and often sounds unnatural. It is usually better to use the active form or a different tense.

Form: has / have + been + being + past participle

(Awkward) The report has been being written since last week.

Better alternative (Active): Someone has been writing the report since last week.

Better alternative (Present Perfect Passive): The report has been in progress for a week.
